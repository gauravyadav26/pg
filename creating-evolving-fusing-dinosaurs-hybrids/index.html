Jurassic World Alive: Creating, Evolving and Fusing Dinosaurs (Hybrids)

Gaurav Yadav

December 16, 2022
Jurassic World Alive

In the popular mobile game Jurassic World Alive, players have the opportunity to create, evolve, and fuse their own dinosaurs. This feature adds a level of strategy and customization to the game, as players must carefully choose which dinosaurs to create, evolve, and fuse in order to build the strongest team possible.

To create a new dinosaur, players must collect DNA samples from different species of dinosaurs. These DNA samples can be obtained by finding them in the wild, purchasing them with in-game currency, or completing special events and challenges. Once a player has collected enough DNA samples, they can use them to create a new dinosaur in the laboratory.

Hybrids are generally stronger than their normal counterparts and can have more skills, as they can have multiple skills of the parent dino.

To understand hybrids better, let’s first have a basic understanding of wild spawns and how are dinos created and evolved in Jurassic World Alive.

Wild Spawns and Creating Dinos

Players can encounter all the Dinosaurs in the wild and collect DNA from them. But as the rarity of the Dino increases the amount of DNA collected will decrease. For example, if a player is at level 6, they get 16 DNA for a direct hit on a common Dino, 12 for a rare, and 11 for epic.

On top of that, the amount of DNA required to create a Dinosaur also increases with the rarity

  • Common Dinos will require a total of 50 DNA to create;
  • Rare Dinos will require a total of 100 DNA to create;
  • Epic Dinos will require a total of 150 DNA  to create.

Players can also collect DNA from incubators, which are really helpful in collecting DNA for epic-level Dinos. Creating A dinosaur does not cost any coins.

Steps to create a Dino:

  • Select the dinosaurs you want to create,
  • Under the Dino avatar, there will be a create button and it will turn green if you have the required DNA to create that dino.

Evolving

Once you have created a Dino, you can now evolve it to make it stronger. But to evolve a dino you need DNA and coins and the cost of evolution keeps on increasing as the level of the dino increases.

Evolving a dinosaur increases its health and damage stats only other stats are not affected.

Why evolving a dino important?

Evolving a dinosaur makes it stronger and more useful in battle and to create a hybrid, and a hybrid can only be created if the parent dino is of the required level.

Steps to Evolve:

  • Select the dinosaurs you want to evolve,
  • Under the Dino avatar, there will be a evolve button and it will turn green if you have the required DNA and coins to evolve it.

Fusing (Hybrids)

Fusing is another way to create Dinosaurs in Jurassic World Alive, these dinos are called hybrids. To create a hybrid you need two specific adequately evolved Dinos and their DNA. Fusing dinos to create hybrids cost parent dinos DNA and coins.

For Example, to create a Nodopatosaurs (a hybrid dino), Apatosaurus and Nodosaurus should be at least level 10 before you can fuse them. Then you need adequate DNA of both the dinos, in this case, 50 DNA and 100 coins to fuse them and create Nodopatosaurs DNA. The amount of DNA created is random and can be anything from 10 to 90. Once to have created 100 Nodopatosaurs DNA, you can create that hybrid.

Once you have created a hybrid dino, it can be evolved by creating DNA through the same process.

Below we have a hybrid of Einiosaurus and Nundasuchus, Einiasuchus. It can be evolved by fusing and creating DNA from the parents.

The levels of dinos needed and amount of coins vary depending on the rarity of the hybrid you’re trying to produce:

  • Rare hybrid – Level 5 dinosaurs and 20 coins
  • Epic hybrid – Level 10 dinosaurs and 100 coins
  • Legendary hybrid – Level 15 dinosaurs and 200 coins

Hybrid List and requirements

  • Allosinosaurus- A hybrid of the Allosaurus and the Sinoceratops. This is a legendary dinosaur and it takes 200 gold coins for each DNA fusion.
  • Ankyntrosaurus – A hybrid of the Ankylosaurus and the Kentrosaurus. Another legendary and this one will take 70 DNA from each dinosaur and 200 coins each time you fuse the DNA.
  • Amargocephalus – A hybrid of the Amargasaurus and the Euplocephalus. This is an epic dinosaur and it will take 100 gold coins and will take 70 DNA from the Amargasurus and 220 DNA from the Euplocephalus.
  • Gigaspikasaur – A hybrid of the Amargasaurus and the Nodopatosaurus.
  • Einiasuchus – A hybrid of the Einiosaurus and the Nundasuchus. This one is only a rare dinosaur and it takes 20 gold coins to fuse if you have the DNA.
  • Gorgosuchus – A hybrid of the Gorgosaurus and the Kaprosuchus. This is another epic dinosaur and it will take 100 coins plus the DNA from both to fuse.
  • Majundasuchus – A hybrid of the Majungasaurus and the Nundasuchus. This dinosaur is only rare and will take 20 gold coins to fuse with the dino DNA.
  • Megalosuchus – A hybrid of the Megalosaurus and the Gorgosuchus. This is a legendary dinosaur and will take 200 gold coins and 70 Megalosaurus DNA and 50 Gorgosuchus DNA.
  • Magnapyritor – A hybrid of the Dimetrodon and the Pyrritator. This one is classified as Unique and costs 1000 gold coins per fusion plus all the dinosaur DNA.
  • Nodopatosaurus – A hybrid of the Nodosaurus and the Apatosaurus. Another epic dinosaur and this one only takes 100 gold coins and 220 Nodosaurus DNA and 70 Apatosaurus DNA.
  • Paramoloch – A hybrid of the Parasaurolophus and the Stygimoloch. The Paramoloch is a legendary dinosaur and will take you 200 gold coins to fuse plus plenty of dino DNA.
  • Postimetrodon – A hybrid of the Postosuchus and the Dimetrodon. The Postimetrodon is only an epic so it will run you 100 gold and plenty of DNA.
  • Indoraptor – A hybrid of Indominus Rex and Velociraptor. It’s a Unique Dino and requires 50 DNA of INdominus Rex, 2000 DNA of Velociraptor, and 1000 coins per fusion.

How to find possible hybrids

Just visit the dinosaur collection, if you collected or even encountered a dino in wild, you will see a section called ‘Possible Hybrids.’ Simply scroll down below the evolution section and you’ll find it right above the Stats.

If you haven’t a dino, the options here will show up as ‘Unknown’ and show the Jurassic World logo. However, if you have encountered a dino, you will see a picture of the dinosaur and be able to tap on it to view it.

Why are hybrids better than normal dinosaurs

Hybrids are cool but are also stronger and more useful in battle as they have a couple of things going for them.

The first one is that they don’t start at level 1, so as soon as you create them they are useful in higher-level arenas.

The other useful hybrid trait is that they appear to have more abilities, as many as four. That obviously means you’ll have more options in each round of a battle and likely will have something other than the basic attack that isn’t on its cooldown at any given time.
